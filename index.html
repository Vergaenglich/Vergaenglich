Beauty is everywhere, if you slow down enough to notice it. In the decay and the rush, in the quiet street and the crowded square, in the remarkable face you pass without a second thought. About

Photography as an act of noticing and remembering

This work is an appreciation for society, for the value of time, for the weight of a single moment that deserves to be admired. I'm an expath from Belgium living in Berlin with Italian roots. I try to capture things in the way they are.

Vergänglich was the only honest name. The act of capturing something beautiful is inseparable from the awareness that it's already gone. Shot across Berlin, Chicago, Havana, Belgium, and Black and white, colour, grain and stillness. Every image is an argument against forgetting, and proof that joy is everywhere if you look.

The camera is a Fujifilm Xpro-2, and the imperfections are intentional.
